    • About Me
    I am Saurabh Jha, a research staff member at IBM Research at Yorktown Heights, NY (since 2021). I received my Ph.D. in 2021 and MS in 2016 in Computer Science from the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign (UIUC). I received my B.Tech from VIT University. Before joining UIUC, I was a visiting scholar at NTU Singapore. In the past, I have interned at IBM (2019), NVIDIA Research (2018, 2020), and Microsoft Research (2014, 2015).

    I work at the intersection of Machine Learning and Systems. My research has contributed to the integration of several innovative features into products deployed at scale. Notably, my work on probable root cause identification (SC 2020) and GenAI observability was integrated into Instana, IBM’s observability platform used by thousands of businesses, and deployed on several HPC systems. Currently, I am focused on developing the orchestration framework for multi-modal AI inference systems, which will be deployed with IBM Watsonx, as well as the orchestration framework for multi-agent systems.

    I have recieved several awards, most notably the IBM Ph.D. fellowship (2020-2021), SAP Industry Ph.D. scholarship (2014), and the VIT University best outgoing student award (2014). My work has resulted several best paper awards and honorable mentions in prestigious international conferences such as ACM/IEEE SC 2020, IEEE ISSRE 2020, and ACM HPDC 2013. I received the 2024 IBM Tech award that recognizes the top technical IBMers.
