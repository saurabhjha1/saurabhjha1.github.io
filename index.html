    • About Me
    I am Saurabh Jha, a research staff member at IBM Research at Yorktown Heights, NY (since 2021). I received my Ph.D. in 2021 and MS in 2016 in Computer Science from the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign (UIUC), where I worked with Prof. Ravishankar K. Iyer and Prof. Zbigniew Kalbarczyk in the DEPEND research group in CSL. I received my B.Tech from VIT University. Before joining UIUC, I worked as a visiting scholar at NTU Singapore where I worked with Prof. Bingsheng He on accelerating database queries on heterogeneous processors. In the past, I have interned at IBM (2019), NVIDIA Research (2018, 2020), and Microsoft Research (2014, 2015).

    My current research interests include design and assessment of self-driven autonomous systems (such as self-driving cars) and extreme scale systems (such as hybrid cloud and high-peformance computing). I work at the intersection of Machine Learning (with special interest in causal and generative models) and Systems (with special focus on dependability).

    I have published papers in prestigious international journals (such as TDSC, TOR and PVLDB) and conferences (such as VLDB, NSDI, SuperComputing, ICS, OSDI, ASPLOS, DSN, ISSRE and ICML). I have received number of awards, most notably IBM Ph.D. fellowship (2020-2021), SAP Industry Ph.D. scholarship (2014) and the VIT University best outgoing student award (2014). I have recieved number of paper awards and honorable mentions in international prestigious conferences such as ISSRE 2020, SC 2020 and HPDC 2013.
